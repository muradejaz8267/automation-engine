C. 67%
D. 90%

Explanation:

* Sensitivity = 95%, Specificity = 90%

Why distractors are incorrect:
   * A: 2% = pretest probability, not PPV

   * C/D/E: Overestimates PPV; PPV is strongly influenced by low prevalence

Question 2
A randomized controlled trial evaluates a new antihypertensive drug. The study reports: incidence of stroke 4% in the treatment group versus 8% in the control group.
Which metric best quantifies the absolute decrease in risk?
A. Relative risk
B. Relative risk reduction
C. Absolute risk reduction
D. Number needed to harm
E. Odds ratio
Correct Answer: C
Explanation:
      * Absolute risk reduction (ARR) = Control event rate – Experimental event rate = 8% – 4% = 4%

      * Relative risk and RRR are proportions; NNH is for harms; odds ratio is a ratio of odds, not risk difference.
