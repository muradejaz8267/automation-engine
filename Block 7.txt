Question 1
A 28-year-old G2P1 woman at 34 weeks gestation presents with severe headache, visual disturbances, and epigastric pain. BP is 172/112 mm Hg. Labs show AST 140 U/L, ALT 130 U/L, platelets 85,000/µL, and LDH elevated. Fetal monitoring shows late decelerations.
What is the most appropriate next step in management?
Answer Choices:
(A) Oral labetalol and observation
(B) IV magnesium sulfate only
(C) Emergent cesarean delivery after stabilization
(D) Induction of labor with oxytocin
