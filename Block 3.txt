Question 1
A 67-year-old man with a history of hypertension, diabetes, and prior myocardial infarction presents with progressive dyspnea on exertion and orthopnea. Physical exam reveals bibasilar crackles, elevated JVP, and an S3 gallop. Echocardiography shows an ejection fraction of 30% with global hypokinesis.
Which medication has been shown to provide the greatest mortality benefit in this patient?
Answer Choices:
(A) Loop diuretic
(B) Digoxin
(C) ACE inhibitor
(D) Nitrate therapy
(E) Calcium channel blocker
Correct Answer: C
Explanation:
In HFrEF, ACE inhibitors (or ARBs/ARNIs) provide a clear mortality benefit by reducing afterload and neurohormonal activation. Diuretics improve symptoms only.
