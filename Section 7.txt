Question 1
A 3-year-old boy is brought to the clinic for evaluation of persistent drooling, difficulty swallowing, and hoarseness. Physical examination reveals midline neck swelling that moves with swallowing, and the mass is located anterior to the thyrohyoid membrane. There is a history of recurrent upper respiratory infections.
Which embryologic structure is most directly implicated in the formation of this lesion?
A. Second pharyngeal arch
B. First pharyngeal pouch
C. Thyroglossal duct
D. Lateral cervical sinus
E. Third pharyngeal pouch
Correct Answer: C
Explanation:
 A thyroglossal duct cyst results from incomplete involution of the thyroglossal duct, which is a midline structure connecting the developing thyroid to the foramen cecum. Moves with swallowing due to attachment to hyoid bone. Other pouches/arches lead to branchial cleft cysts or parathyroid/tonsillar anomalies.
